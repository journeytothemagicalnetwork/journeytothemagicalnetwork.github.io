She appears on a [[Chocolate Frog Card]].<ref name=\"PSg\" />\n\n==Biography==\nLaverne de Montmorency was born in [[1823]], somewhere in the [[Great Britain|British Isles]].<ref name=\"PSg\">{{PS|G}}</ref>\n\nIn her youth, she attended [[Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry]] from around [[1834]]-[[1841]], and was [[Sorting ceremony|Sorted]] into [[Ravenclaw]].<ref name=\"pottermore\"/>\n\nAfter her graduation from [[Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry|Hogwarts School]], Laverne de Montmorency presumably studied further into [[Potion|Potion-making]], becoming an accomplished [[potioneer]] and an expert in [[Love Potion|love potions]]. She became famous for her invention of many such potions.<ref name=\"PSg\" />\n\nMs. de Montmorency died in [[1893]], aged around 70. In her honour, her invention of many [[Love Potion|love potions]] was recorded on a Chocolate Frog Card.\n\nOn [[1 September]], [[1991]], [[Prefect]] [[Robert Hilliard]] welcomed the [[Ravenclaw]] [[first year]]s to [[Ravenclaw Tower]] with a short speech, in which he mentioned [[potioneer]] Laverne de Montmorency as one of the many notable Hogwarts students, who had been [[Sorting ceremony|Sorted]], like them, into Ravenclaw.<ref name=\"pottermore\"/>\n\n==Magical abilities and skills==\n*'''[[Potion]]s''': Laverne was a highly accomplished professional [[Potioneer]] and was thus highly proficient in the art and [[science]] of potion-making, having successfully invented a number of [[Love Potion]]s in her lifetime. Given how Love Potions are particularly advanced brews, and that she was even featured on a [[Chocolate Frog Card]] for her impressive achievements, this shows her great level of expertise and talent in this branch of [[magic]].\n\n==Etymology==\n*\"Laverne\" is derived from a French place-name that includes the Gaulish word ''vern, ''meaning \"[[alder]].\" It is sometimes associated with [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laverna Laverna], the Roman goddess of thieves, or the Latin adjective ''vernus, ''meaning \"of spring.\"<ref>https://www.behindthename.com/name/laverne</ref>\n*The {{wplink|House of Montmorency}} was one of the oldest and most distinguished noble families in France. [[Wikipedia:Montmorency, Val-d'Oise|Montmorency]] was also the name of the family's fiefdom.
